------------------------------------------------------------

HTML Entities Reference Table (Most Useful Symbols)

------------------------------------------------------------

Reserved Characters

&lt; < Less-than sign

&gt; > Greater-than sign

&amp; & Ampersand

&quot; " Double quote

&apos; ' Apostrophe (single quote)

Spaces

&nbsp; (non-breaking space) Prevents line break

&ensp; (en space) Wider than normal space

&emsp; (em space) Extra wide space

&thinsp; (thin space) Narrower than normal space

Currency

&cent; ¢ Cent sign

&pound; £ Pound sterling

&yen; ¥ Japanese yen

&euro; € Euro

&dollar; $ Dollar (alternative to typing $ directly)

Math and Logic

&plus; + Plus sign

&minus; − Minus sign

&times; × Multiplication

&divide; ÷ Division

&equals; = Equals sign

&ne; ≠ Not equal

&le; ≤ Less than or equal

&ge; ≥ Greater than or equal

&plusmn; ± Plus/minus

Punctuation and Marks

&copy; © Copyright

&reg; ® Registered trademark

&trade; ™ Trademark

&sect; § Section symbol

&para; ¶ Paragraph symbol

&hellip; … Ellipsis

&bull; • Bullet

&mdash; — Em dash

&ndash; – En dash

Arrows

&rarr; → Right arrow

&larr; ← Left arrow

&uarr; ↑ Up arrow

&darr; ↓ Down arrow

Greek Letters (common in science/crypto)

&alpha; α Alpha

&beta; β Beta

&gamma; γ Gamma

&delta; δ Delta

&pi; π Pi

&sigma; σ Sigma

&theta; θ Theta

&omega; ω Omega

------------------------------------------------------------

Usage Example

------------------------------------------------------------

HTML code:

If x &lt; 10 &amp; y &gt; 20, then &pi; ≈ 3.14&nbsp;&rarr;&nbsp;Done.

Browser output:

If x < 10 & y > 20, then π ≈ 3.14 → Done.
